Integrated Canonizer and Solver

ICS (Integrated Canonizer and Solver) is an efficient decision procedure for a rich fragment of first-order equational logic. Terms are built from uninterpreted functions symbols, and operators from a rich combination of datatypes such as linear arithmetic, arrays, sets, propositional logic, and fixed-sized bitvectors.

The main application areas of ICS are the analysis of hardware and software systems. ICS is also one of the main workhorses underlying the upcoming release 3.0 of the PVS theorem prover. It is available free of charge under the ICS license (in Word format).
